Right now DeckOS requires a ritual. Open tab → authenticate → navigate → interact. Every one of those steps is friction, and friction is the enemy of invisibility. The system is built around you going to it. Invisible systems come to you.

Here's where each "no-interface" channel actually stands:

Voice — 80% there, wrong placement
The TTS/STT infrastructure exists and works. But it only works inside the browser tab. True voice invisibility means you walk into a room and speak — not that you open a website and click a microphone button. The capability is built. The delivery mechanism is wrong.

WhatsApp — 10% there, but highest value
The channel type "whatsapp" is literally in the codebase. The chat API already accepts it and would handle it correctly. But the wire isn't connected — there's no inbound hook receiving WhatsApp messages and routing them to the AI. This is the single highest-leverage invisibility unlock. WhatsApp is already on your phone. You already open it 40 times a day. Once that wire is connected, JARVIS is always one message away with zero interface at all.

Presence — half-built, wrong direction
The presence system detects active/idle/passive but it's reactive — it watches you and updates a UI nobody looks at. For invisibility, presence needs to run in the other direction: JARVIS notices you've been idle and proactively surfaces something useful. Or notices you've left a location and adjusts context. Right now presence informs a dashboard. It should inform behavior.

Mobile — closest to invisible, just needs push
The mobile app is the best candidate for invisibility because it's already on your phone. But it still requires you to open it. One addition — push notifications from JARVIS — and it becomes something that appears when it needs you instead of waiting for you to appear.

What "invisibility" actually requires, in order:

WhatsApp connection — You text from anywhere, JARVIS responds. No DeckOS tab required, ever. The architecture already handles it. This is a connector away.
JARVIS reaches out, not just responds — When the Initiative Engine fires a nudge, it should have an output channel. Right now nudges update a counter on a dashboard. They should become a WhatsApp message or push notification.
Mobile PWA + background push — Make the mobile app installable, add web push. JARVIS can surface things to your phone without you initiating anything.
Voice without the screen — Long-term: wake word or always-listening mode. Short-term: voice note → WhatsApp → JARVIS responds in voice. That's actually achievable with what's built.
The WhatsApp wire is the move. Want me to connect it? Once that's in, DeckOS becomes something you never need to open — you just text a number and JARVIS is there.
