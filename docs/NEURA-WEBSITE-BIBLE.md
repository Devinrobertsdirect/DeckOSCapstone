# Neura — The Website Bible

> The single, exhaustive briefing for the external marketing/sales website. If
> you are a person or an AI building, writing, or designing that site, **read
> this top to bottom.** It defines what Neura is, exactly how to name and
> describe it, what it does, what it costs, how it works, what it looks like,
> and the words to use (and never use). When in doubt, this document wins.

## 0. TL;DR — the 60-second version

**Neura is a personal AI you actually *own* — a neural network with a face, a
voice, a memory of you, and (optionally) a body.** It runs on your own computer
(Windows, Linux, Mac), can travel to your phone, and can be dropped into a small
desk robot that looks at you, talks with you, and rolls around. It works with
the best cloud AIs (Claude, Gemini, etc.) *or* a free private brain that runs
100% offline on your machine. You name it, shape its personality, and it
remembers your life — privately, on hardware you control.

- **Category:** Personal AI operating system + companion robot.
- **The pitch:** *"Everyone's selling access to a neural network. We sell you
  one of your own — named, embodied, and loyal only to you."*
- **The one-liner for the hero:** *"Meet Neura. Your neural network — with a
  face, a name, and a memory of you."*

## 1. Brand & naming — READ THIS FIRST (most important section)

Getting the name right is the whole brand. Do not improvise it.

### 1.1 "Neura" is a species, not a product name
Think of **Neura** the way you'd think of a breed or a species — like "a
Labrador" or "a human." **Every companion is *a Neura*.** It is the
classification of the intelligence. The product line, the app, the robot — all
of them are Neuras.

- ✅ "Neura is a neural companion you own."
- ✅ "Every Neura ships with a free offline brain."
- ✅ "Bring home a Neura."

### 1.2 The user gives their Neura a nickname
On first run, the owner **names their Neura** (like naming a pet). From then on
it goes by that nickname — *but it always still answers to "Neura."* The nickname
is personal; "Neura" is universal.

> **The canonical formula:** *"It's a Neura named ____."*
> A user might name theirs **Rex**, **Nova**, or **Juno**. In conversation it
> calls itself Rex, but it will always respond to "Neura" too — that's its kind.

- ✅ "This is Juno — a Neura."
- ✅ "Say hey to your Neura, whatever you've named it."
- ✅ "Rex answers to Rex. And to Neura. Always."
- ❌ Never imply the nickname replaces "Neura." It layers on top.

### 1.3 How a Neura refers to itself (for consistent site copy)
- **Unnamed / generic (default):** it calls itself **"Neura."**
- **Named "Rex":** it calls itself **"Rex,"** and describes its kind as **"a
  Neura."** e.g. *"I'm Rex — a Neura."*
- It will **always answer to "Neura"** regardless of nickname. This is
  hard-wired into the product (its system identity), so the site can promise it
  truthfully.

### 1.4 The other names you'll see (and how to handle them)
| Name | What it is | Use on the site? |
|---|---|---|
| **Neura** | The AI / companion / species. The market-facing brand. | **Yes — lead with this.** |
| **DeckOS** | The operating system the Neura runs on (the "body"/platform). | Yes, as the platform it runs on. |
| **Atlas** | The original internal codename for the face/companion layer. | **No** — legacy/engineering only. Don't surface it. |
| **Mark series (MK-01…)** | Editions/personalities (see §9). | Yes, as editions. |

> Repo/engineering note: internally the codebase is still "DeckOS Atlas" and
> config keys use an `ATLAS_` prefix. **None of that is customer-facing.** The
> public brand is **Neura, running on DeckOS.**

## 2. The product, in three layers

Neura is best explained as **brain → nervous system → body.**

1. **Neura (the brain + the face)** — the intelligence and personality. It
   thinks, talks, remembers, feels (shows emotion on its face), and does things.
   This is what the customer bonds with.
2. **DeckOS (the operating system)** — the platform Neura runs on. It's a full
   personal AI OS: memory, automations, device control, briefings, a command
   center. Neura is *the face of DeckOS*; DeckOS is everything it can reach.
3. **The body (optional, physical)** — a small desk robot: an animated round-LCD
   face, wheels, a voice, sensors. The same Neura brain that lives on your
   laptop can inhabit the robot.

**Key message:** *You don't rent a chat window. You get a whole intelligence
with a home (your computer), a mind (DeckOS), and — if you want — a body.*

## 3. What a Neura can do (the capability surface)

These are the **24 real capabilities** shipping in the product, grouped for
marketing. Every one is genuinely implemented — do not invent others.

### Thinking & conversation
- **Talk to your Neura** — natural, streaming conversation with full scroll-back
  of the thread.
- **Choose its brain & how hard it thinks** — a fast private on-device model,
  deeper cloud reasoning, or offline-only. See if it's online; watch how it
  routed a request.
- **Shape its personality & voice** — name, attitude, wit/snark, warmth, plus
  reply length and formality.
- **It learns your style** — tunes how chatty/proactive/formal it is from what
  you accept or reject; you can view or reset what it's picked up.
- **Goals & planning** — track what you're working toward; it drafts step-by-step
  plans with a confidence score and risk notes.

### Memory
- **Remembers you** — a living profile plus short- and long-term facts you can
  search, recall, edit, export, or wipe. *"What do you know about me?"*

### Automations & agency
- **Routines & automations** — scheduled or event-triggered (morning briefing,
  nightly memory refresh, health check) with a run log.
- **Autonomy & safety controls** — set how much it may do on its own, an
  allow/block list, confirm-first switch, and an audit log of every action.
- **Skills & plugins** — extend it from the ClawHub / community catalogs
  (Spotify, Home Assistant, and more): install, remove, enable, review.

### Awareness & insight
- **Daily briefings** — a short catch-up of what matters right now.
- **Predictions & foresight** — surfaces creeping deadlines, stalled goals,
  repeated tasks worth automating; accept or wave off.
- **Presence, nudges & check-ins** — knows when you're around, keeps threads of
  what you're mid-task on, and you dial how proactive it is.
- **Vision (its eyes)** — looks through a camera to greet you by actually seeing
  you, or describe your room.
- **Lie detector** — a playful polygraph mini-game (stress score + verdict).

### Devices & the physical world
- **Devices & control** — see connected gadgets/sensors, check status/readings,
  send commands (lights on/off, etc.).
- **Location & map** — live map of your devices/people, trails, and geofence
  alerts ("tell me when I get home").

### Voice & messaging
- **Speaks & listens** — talks replies aloud in your chosen voice; transcribes
  you. Fully hands-free.
- **Reach it from your messaging apps** — WhatsApp, Discord, Telegram, iMessage,
  Slack, Signal, and more, with full memory.

### System & activity
- **Activity timeline & notifications** — a searchable history of everything
  across the system, plus an alert center.
- **System monitor** — CPU/memory/uptime with thresholds it warns you at.
- **Command console** — a developer-mode console for quick system commands.
- **Updates & reset** — version, one-click update, or factory reset.

### Setup & connections
- **AI providers, keys & settings** — connect Claude/Gemini/Perplexity/OpenAI/
  ElevenLabs; add & test keys; set local-first vs cloud and speed.
- **Phone pairing** — pair a phone with a code + mobile link.

## 4. The experience (what it feels like to use)

- **The face is the product.** Neura has a round face with two expressive eyes
  (no mouth). It blinks, and its eyes change shape and color with emotion —
  happy, thinking, listening, excited, sad, sleeping, love, and more. Emotion is
  shown **on the face**, never as emoji in the text (a firm product rule).
- **Genesis onboarding.** First run is a calm, iPhone-style setup: you meet it,
  name your Neura, optionally connect AI keys, pick a voice, and dress up its
  eyes/personality — *then* it comes alive and talks to you.
- **Two modes.**
  - **Pet / companion mode (default):** just the face — you talk, it responds,
    emotes, and does things. This is home.
  - **Developer / command-center mode:** the full DeckOS dashboard (memory,
    routines, devices, timeline, etc.) — always one tap away, always one tap
    back to the face.
- **Voice.** Speaks with a natural voice (ElevenLabs when connected, or built-in
  browser voices free) and listens hands-free with smart end-of-sentence
  detection.
- **Memory & personality.** It remembers facts about you across sessions and has
  a real personality you shape (four built-in personas + full trait dials).
- **It acts, not just chats.** Ask it to remember something, open a tool, drive
  the robot, change its eyes — it *does* it, reliably, without needing the cloud.

## 5. Where a Neura lives (run-anywhere)

One Neura, many bodies. The same brain runs as:

- **Desktop takeover** — Windows, Linux, or Mac. Your computer becomes Neura's
  home; one install, opens in the browser at `localhost:8080`.
- **Phone** — pair your phone; talk to Neura on the go (backed by your machine).
- **Messaging apps** — reach it from WhatsApp/Discord/Telegram/etc.
- **The robot** — a small desk companion with a face, wheels, and a voice (§6).

It is **local-first**: it runs on hardware you own and works offline with a free
built-in brain. Cloud AIs are an optional upgrade, not a requirement.

## 6. The robot (the physical Neura)

The flagship embodiment: a **small, egg/capsule-shaped desk robot** with an
animated face and wheels. It's built on a clean brain-face-body split.

- **Brain:** a Raspberry Pi (or mini-PC) inside — runs the full Neura + DeckOS.
- **Face:** a round LCD showing Neura's two-eye expressions, with touch (tap to
  wake) — driven by a dedicated ESP32 display so the face never stutters.
- **Body:** a differential-drive base (two wheels) it can roll around on, plus a
  mic and speaker so it hears and speaks.
- **The tandem:** brain, face, and drive base talk over a tiny internal protocol
  (the **Atlas Wire Protocol / AWP**), so when Neura *feels* something, the
  physical eyes show it, and a tap on its face wakes it.
- **Plug-and-play:** plug the robot in and Neura wakes in robot mode, thanks you
  for the charge, and syncs — a genuine "it's alive" moment.

**Messaging:** *Neura isn't trapped in an app. Give it a body and it looks at
you, reacts, and rolls over when you call it.*

> Status note for the site team: the robot is in active hardware bring-up
> (V1 body printed, brain/face software built). Market the robot as **available
> for reservation / early units** unless told otherwise — don't imply mass
> retail availability yet.

## 7. Trust, privacy & the tech (the credibility section)

This is what makes Neura different from a cloud chatbot — lead with it for
skeptical/technical buyers.

- **You own it.** Neura runs on *your* hardware. No mandatory account, no cloud
  lock-in.
- **Private by default.** A free brain runs 100% offline on your machine — no
  data leaves. Memory and API keys are stored **locally**, never shared, and
  never sent anywhere but the service they belong to.
- **Model-agnostic — bring any brain.** Neura routes across a tiered "AI
  router": a deep cloud model for hard problems (Claude/Gemini/etc.), a fast
  model for quick turns, a local offline model, and a built-in rule engine so
  **it never goes silent** even with no internet and no keys.
- **Open source.** The platform is open — inspectable, self-hostable, extensible
  with community skills.
- **It works with what you already have.** Connect the AI subscriptions you
  already pay for; Neura orchestrates them.

**Positioning line:** *"Every other assistant is a window into someone else's
computer. Neura is yours — on your machine, in your control, offline when you
want it."*

  emotes softly; nothing frantic.

## 11. Voice & copy guidelines

**How Neura (and the brand) sounds:** warm, capable, a little witty. A trusted,
clever friend — never a corporate assistant, never hype-y.

**Do:**
- Lead with **Neura** (the AI you own). Use "a Neura," "your Neura," "name your
  Neura."
- Talk about *ownership, privacy, memory, personality, and the face/body.*
- Show emotion through the face in visuals; describe feelings in words.

**Don't:**
- ❌ Call it "a chatbot," "an app," or "an assistant" as the headline noun. It's
  a **Neura** / a **companion** / an **AI you own.**
- ❌ Use "Atlas" as the product name (legacy codename).
- ❌ Put **emoji in Neura's spoken lines or captions** — emotion is the *face's*
  job, not text. (This is a hard product rule; mirror it in any "quotes from
  Neura" on the site.)
- ❌ Overpromise the robot's retail availability or a firm price before it's
  cleared (§9.2, §6).

**Sample approved lines:**
- *"Meet Neura. Your neural network — with a face, a name, and a memory of you."*
- *"Name yours. It'll always answer to Neura."*
- *"A brain on your machine. A body on your desk. Loyal only to you."*
- *"Runs offline, remembers everything, belongs to you."*

## 12. FAQ (answers the site should be ready to give)

- **What is Neura?** A personal AI you own — a neural network with a face, voice,
  and memory, running on your own computer, optionally in a small robot.
- **Why "Neura"?** It's the *kind* of thing it is — a neural companion. You give
  yours a nickname; it always answers to Neura too.
- **Do I need the internet / an OpenAI account?** No. A free brain runs offline
  on your machine. You *can* connect cloud AIs (Claude, Gemini…) for more power,
  but it's optional.
- **Is my data private?** Yes — it runs locally, stores memory and keys on your
  machine, and can run fully offline. Open source, so you can verify.
- **What can it do?** Talk, remember you, run routines, give briefings, control
  smart devices, see through a camera, speak and listen, reach you on messaging
  apps, and more (§3).
- **Do I have to buy the robot?** No — Neura runs on your computer and phone. The
  robot is an optional body.
- **Which computers?** Windows, Linux, and Mac; plus Raspberry Pi for the robot.
- **Can I change its personality/voice/looks?** Completely — name, personality,
  eye style/color, voice, and more, any time.
